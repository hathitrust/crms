Special Use Cases

 
Case No.
Special Use Case
Instructions
Comments on Note Field
Notes Examples
1s
Title page/verso says (literally) “published simultaneously” in another country

Consider US work and check for Copyright notice, renewal (Use Cases 1/2/3 above)
Note Country and pub is simultaneous
MISC: British pub simultaneous
2s
Title page/verso indicates work is a translation of a foreign publication
Treat as foreign pub (Use Cases 10/11/12 above) but note as translation
Note Translation and place of original pub (foreign title if known)
TRANSLATION: Spain “San Cristobal de la Habana”
3s
The work is a University of Michigan dissertation
Ignore requirement for copyright notice – just check for renewal
N/A
N/A
4s
Title page/verso indicates work is part of an numbered Monographic Series
Check books for copyright notice and renewal of monograph title as usual. If renewed code ic/ren.  If no notice or not renewed, code und/nfi.  
Note title of Series and series number
SERIES/SERIAL: Annals of the American Academy of Political and Science v. 216
5s
Title page/verso indicates work is part of an unnumbered Monographic Series
Check for copyright notice and renewal of monograph title. Don’t worry about series title.
N/A
N/A
6s
Title page/verso indicates work is a Serial, i.e., extracted volume cataloged as a monograph
Code these und/nfi.
Note serial info: (title of serial, vol. and issue, if known.). Note if item is also a collection of articles, etc.
SERIES/SERIAL: Assoc. of Casualty and Surety Co.; ED 14, no. 3
7s
Title page/verso indicates collection of materials or contains additional copyrighted material
Check for renewal of primary piece/collection title. If renewed, code ic/ren. If not renewed, code und/nfi (except architectural works which weren’t covered under copyright laws until 1990).

Note Collection and type of works and any info on tp/verso about copyright of individual works.

Possible eg’s: Artwork, Trade or Society Papers, Poetry, Plays

COLLECTION: articles from Foreign Affairs

COLLECTION: reproductions of copyrighted artwork
8s
Title page/verso includes statement regarding international copyright, e.g., reference to Berne Convention or International Copyright Union
Look for evidence that work was previously published outside the U.S., otherwise, ignore
N/A
N/A
9s
Title page/verso includes copyright statement referencing U.S. Alien Property Custodian
Consider these non-US works
N/A
N/A
10s
Barcode does not match bibliographic information
Code these und/nfi and report to: MDPCorrections@umich.edu
Note that wrong bib record is attached to the work and that you reported it.
WRONG RECORD: Wrong bib record attached to work; reported
11s
Photocopy, e.g., Univ. Microfilms
Base on date of original work, because a photocopy is just a copy
Give original date of publication
MISC: Orig. pub date 1950
12s
UN Documents
Most not in copyright but some are, and status can be difficult to determine. Confirm published in US and if yes, check for copyright notice AND renewal. If you find BOTH, make it und/nfi and note what was found.
Note “Misc” and that it is or appears to be a “UN Doc.” Report presence of copyright notice and anything you find in Stanford database. Anne will look at these case by case.   
MISC: UN Doc (copyright notice/renewal found)  
13s
US Government Documents (normally it states published by the GPO – Government Publishing Office - though some published by depts.)

Gov Docs are just checked like other works.

But if it looks like a Gov Doc and it has a copyright notice and renewal, code it und/nfi and add the renewal info.  

If und/nfi (either because of renewal or checker uncertainty), make a “Misc” note questioning its copyright status.

MISC: US Gov Doc?

MISC: US Gov DOC with renewal?
14s
Title page/verso indicates whole work is a straight reprint of a previously published work (NOT a new edition, e.g., updated version of the original, and/or with a new preface or intro added).

Use the original publication to make a determination.

See case no. 9 for partial reprints.
